🌊 Padma River Desiccation Mapping (2001–2025)¶

Tracking 25 Years of Surface Water Change in Rajshahi Using Python & Google Earth Engine¶

¶

Author: Imtiaj Iqbal Mahfuj

📝 Overview¶

This project investigates the desiccation of the Padma River over a 25-year period (2001–2025), focusing on the Rajshahi region of Bangladesh. The Padma River, one of the most critical river systems in South Asia, has shown significant hydrological and morphological changes in recent decades due to both natural and anthropogenic pressures.

By leveraging MODIS Surface Reflectance data (MOD09A1), this study computes the Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) annually to assess temporal changes in surface water extent.

The main objectives include:

  • Quantifying long-term changes in the Padma River’s surface water.
  • Visualizing annual variations in NDWI to identify desiccation trends.
  • Demonstrating how open-source geospatial Python tools can be used for time-series river analysis.

This analysis is relevant for hydrologists, climate researchers, planners, and policy-makers seeking to understand river health, climate impacts, and sustainable water management strategies.

🛠️ Tools & Technologies¶

⚙️ Methodology¶

Step Description
1. Data Collection MODIS Surface Reflectance (MOD09A1) from Google Earth Engine (2001–2025).
2. Preprocessing Selected NIR (sur_refl_b02) and Green (sur_refl_b04) bands, clipped to Rajshahi AOI.
3. Analysis Computed NDWI = (Green - NIR) / (Green + NIR) for each timestep; aggregated annually.
4. Visualization Used xarray and matplotlib to plot 25 annual NDWI frames showing water area changes.

📊 Results & Insights¶

  • 🔹 Clear decline in water presence along the Padma River in the Rajshahi region from 2001 to 2025.
  • 🔹 NDWI trends show progressive desiccation of floodplains and reduction in seasonal water coverage.
  • 🔹 Visual evidence of riverbank erosion and shifting river channels over two decades.

❓ Why this project?¶

  • The Padma River plays a key role in Bangladesh’s hydrology and ecosystem stability, yet systematic long-term analyses of its desiccation have been limited.
  • This project bridges that gap by offering temporal mapping and visualization using open-source geospatial tools.
  • Insights can inform river basin management, climate adaptation, and policy planning for sustainable river conservation.

  • 🌊 River desiccation mapping and trend visualization

  • 🧾 Ready-to-use workflow for hydrological change detection
